Dnd Character Creator - 5 Edition

Tool to help create a and manage a dnd character.

This tool is intended to help beginners create their first character and walk people through the process of character creation.

The #1 rule for dnd is to create something that you can be excited about and have fun playing, not to make the mose over-powered character you can create. After all - the goal of the game is to have fun, not to gain advantages over your team mates.

Choose your class first. This will help decide your characters race. I choose to do this step first because a characters class is a bigger deciding factor to the style of play your character will follow.

Class list:

  • Barbarian - Run at people
  • Bard - Supports people with song
  • Cleric - Healer from diety
  • Druid - Tree Hugger/Zoo Summoner
  • Fighter - Average Soldier with average skills
  • Monk - Martial arts/Spiritual person
  • Paladin - Death to all Evil
  • Ranger - Robin hood (shoot at people)
  • Rogue - Criminal, basic fiend
  • Sorcerer - Magic from bloodline
  • Warlock - Magic from deity
  • Wizard - Magic from books

Class Features

  • Rage - like the hulk. You can get mad on your turn. Can't Rage if you have heavy armor. The following applies:
    • Advantage on strength checks and saving throws.
    • When you attack(melee), you get bonus damage (increases with barbarian level)
    • Resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slash damage.
    • Can't cast spells
    • Rage lasts for 1 minute. Ends early if you are knocked out, or can't attack anything.
    • Can only rage once until you rest at lvl 1.
  • Unarmored Defense - while you aren't wearing armor, your AC(armor class) is 10 + dexterity modifier + constitution modifier. (shields are ok)
  • Spell replace - When you level up, you can replace a spell you have with one from the Bard spell list up to your max level.
  • Spellcasting Ability - Uses charisma.
  • Ritual Casting - Any bard spell you know can be cast as a ritual.
  • Spellcasting Focus - Use a musical instrument for spellcasting focus for bard spells.
  • Bardic Inspiration - Use this on a teammate to "inspire" them. They get a + 1d6 to use for any ability check within the next 10 minutes. (can use it after you roll the 1d20). You can use this at max 1 or your chrisma modifier.
  • Use Wisdom modifier for Spells
  • Ritual Casting - Cast a cleric spell as a ritual if prepared and spell has ritual tag.
